Occupation and education

For the past 19 years, my professional career has revolved around machining and welding various types of metals, as well as constructing steel and aluminum structures. I began my career as an assistant blacksmith, and currently, I utilize technical documentation to fabricate and weld steel devices used in the medical industry. Additionally, I have completed engineering studies at the Faculty of Mechanical Engineering, specializing in programming CNC machine tools and devices. During my time at the University of Technology in Koszalin, I had the opportunity to work with CAD and CAM software, which supported the design and manufacturing process of objects using CNC devices. One of my most memorable experiences was working with the Solidworks program. Presently, I have embarked on the Frontend Developer from Scratch course as I am deeply interested in pursuing a career in IT.

Goals and dreams

I have been working for the same company for approximately 7 years, where I am involved in the production of equipment for the medical industry. Despite completing courses in "coded welding" and "responsible welding coordination" a few years ago, I don't see any significant opportunities for further development in this field. However, I have developed a strong interest in the IT industry due to its abundant growth prospects, remote work possibilities, and competitive salaries. Currently, I am attending a frontend developer course and I have discovered a great deal of satisfaction and motivation in programming, which fuels my desire for continuous improvement. Moreover, transitioning into a programming career would also bring health benefits since, as a welder, I am consistently exposed to various fumes, dust, and chemical fluids. My primary goal at the moment is to successfully complete the IT course. Additionally, I plan to acquire knowledge in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, and other related fields, while expanding my portfolio as much as possible. Ultimately, my dream is to secure a position as a junior developer, paving the way for a new and fulfilling career. Although I have been working as a welder-fabricator in the engineering field for 20 years, I aspire to dedicate the next 20 years (or more) to honing my skills in the IT industry.
